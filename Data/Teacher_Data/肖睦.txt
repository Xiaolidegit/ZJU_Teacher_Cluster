In normal epithelial cells TGF-beta inhibits cell growth and promoters cell apoptosis; on the other hand it functions as a major inducer in EMT (Epithelial to Mesenchymal transition). Thus in the early stage of cancer development TGF-beta as a cancer suppressor inhibits normal epithelial cell growth but in the latterly stage TGF-beta could promoter tumor metastasis. However how TGF-beta switch its function in Cancer development is not very clear. Smads proteins as key transcriptional factors in TGF-beta signaling have been well studied.We use a combinatorial approach of high-throughput RNA sequencing and functional genomic screen to identify1. The novel lncRNAs in controlling TGF-beta/Smad signal transduction.2. RNA splicing and post-transcriptional regulation in TGF-beta/Smad signal transduction.  Molecular mechanisms and pathogenesis of osteoporosis osteoarthritis intervertebral disc degeneration and fracture healingThe molecular mechanism of adult mesenchymal stem cells during skeletal repair.Adult progenitor stem cells (MSCs) have enormous potential for bone tissue repair and regeneration. I studied the detail mechanism of the signaling pathways such as TGF-beta and BMP in activation proliferation and differentiation of mesenchymal stem cells and progenitor cells during skeletal repair. RNA modification and RNA splicing1. Functionality and substrate specificity of human box H/ACA guide RNA                        A large number of box H/ACA RNAs have been identified in human cells and have been predicted to account for nearly all pseudouridylation sites in human rRNAs. However the function of these human H/ACA RNAs in directing pseudouridylation has been verified experimentally in only two cases. We used in vitro and in vivo reconstitution systems including yeast and mammalian systems to test the function of human H/ACA RNAs and found a lot of very important motif.2. Environmental stress and signaling pathway (Tor1) induced RNA modification and its reversal?                                                                                                       In nature environmental factors like temperature and nutrient availability are continuously changing. It is an intrinsic property of living organisms to sense and properly respond to environmental changes. By pulling-down endogenous U2 snRNA we found starvation will induce a new pseudouridylation in Yeast U2 snRNA by inactivating Tor1 signaling pathway. And new nutrients can slightly reverse the pseudouridylation by activating potential proteins.3. Noncoding RNA and gene regulation snoRNP can guide modification of rRNA and snRNA. Our works found snoRNAs could directly guide the modification of mRNA; and then regulate mRNA stability and/or change RNA codon. RNA Structure and folding in RNA splicing1. RNA structure and folding of Candida group I intron.I studied RNA structure and folding of Candida group I intron and found RNA folding of Candida group I intron significantly differs from the typical Group I Intron. The new RNA interaction motifs were identified which stabilize RNA tertiary structure and contribute to Splicing. Disruption of the RNA interaction will increase the first step of transesterification but decrease the secondary step in Candida group I intron Self-splicing.2.
