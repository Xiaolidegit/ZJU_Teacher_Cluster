Cambridge Institute of Language Research.研究方向:My research interests are in the theory of grammar specifically sentence structure (syntax) and interpretation (semantics information structure and so on). My work investigates the principles and regularities underlying the surface variation within and between human languages. Phenomena I have worked on include cleft and copular constructions relative clauses anaphoric binding negative polarity items agreement and extraction from noun phrases. I have published a monograph entitled Clefts and Their Relatives (2012 John Benjamins) and articles in journals including Lingua Natural Language & Linguistic Theory and Syntax.A great advantage of working at ZJU is that I am able to work on Chinese and take advantage of both native-speaker knowledge and linguists expertise. With Ying Liu (刘莹) I am working on the syntax and semantics of Mandarin shi...de (是...的) constructions and on the similarities and differences between them and English it-clefts. With my Masters student Jiaxin Feng (冯嘉昕) I am working on Mandarin constructions involving passive bei (被) and disposal ba (把) as compared with English constructions involving have and get. Other topics on which I am working include the syntax of double object constructions in Mandarin and Cantonese and the syntax of questions in Xiaoshan (萧山) Chinese (a Wu variety).教学与课程:现代语言学（本科）语义学（硕士）句法学（本科）
