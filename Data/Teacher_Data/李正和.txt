Although plant negative-strand RNA (NSR) viruses consist of members in the Rhabdoviridae Bunyaviridae Ophioviridae families and several unassigned genera that collectively account for many economically important crop diseases unfortunately several technical difficulties have hindered application of genetic engineering to these groups of plant viruses. We developed the first reverse genetics system for any plant NSR viruses. Using a model plant rhabdovirus - sonchus yellow net virus (SYNV) an efficient procedure was described to produce infectious virus from cloned cDNA plasmids and to engineer recombinant SYNV vector for stable expression of foreigner genes. Inspired by the initial successes with SYNV we are currently developing additional suitable in vivo transcription systems and reverse genetics approaches for other plant nucleorhabdoviruses and cytorhabdoviruses of economic importance as well as segmented plant negative-stranded RNA viruses.(2) Biology of Plant RhabdovirusesAlthough plant rhabdoviruses pose significant threats to agriculture our understanding of their infection cycles pathogenesis and insect transmission has lagged behind many other types of plant viruses largely because of the inability to implement genetic engineering studies. The goal of our studies is to address the roles of viral and host proteins in virus movement budding virion morphogenesis insect transmission. Currently we have been working with three different rhabdoviruses i.e. sonchus yellow net nucleorhadovirus and two cytorhabdoviruses tomato yellow mottle-associated virus and rice stripe mosaic virus. We are combining reverse genetics cell biology and biochemical approaches to address mechanisms and molecular events leading to productive rhabdovirus infection and spreading.
